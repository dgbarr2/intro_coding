An Introduction to Coding in R

Introduction

The current Foundation Year course for new graduates includes an introductory course on coding with R. All Bank staff can download the materials for the course from this page. As of June 2020 about 70% of the core course material is available; the remainder will be completed before September. Thereafter, we intend to add new material covering additional topics in response to staff requests.

We keep it simple at the start...

  • The first 4 topics are designed for those who have never written code before.
  • Topics 5 and 6 explain how to load and graph data.

...then introduce some applications...

  • Estimate the relationship between money and inflation using some simple regressions.
  • Illustrate the way in which economists explain risk aversion.
  • Calculate 2 standard risk measures: variance and Value at Risk
  • Illustrate the benefits of portfolio diversification for risk management.
  • Explain the relationship between the yield and price of a bond.
  • Introduce Monte Carlo simulation as an intuitive approach to asset pricing.

...using these course materials.

  • Slide packs designed for self-study.
  • Executable R code files, the contents of which are explained line by line in the slides.
  • Brief discussion of the theory underlying the applications (not as a substitute for an economics or finance course however).
  • Study advice in the introductory slides - learn by doing!
  • Topics arranged into self-contained boxes (see below) which should each take about an hour to work through.
  • Please click the links in the boxes below to download the course materials.
